Abstract

The Sustainable Structural Compliance Ontology (SSCO) is a modular ontology developed to support sustainability-oriented compliance checking in structural engineering within the Architecture, Engineering, Construction, and Operation (AECO) domain. It provides a shared semantic framework for representing structural information, analysis evidence, sustainability requirements, and compliance outcomes in an integrated and traceable way. The ontology is intended to support the connection of Building Information Modeling (BIM)-based project information, engineering assessment evidence derived from Finite Element Method (FEM)-based structural analysis, and requirement statements used in compliance checking. SSCO enables graph-based knowledge representation and machine-readable querying for compliance-related tasks. It is designed to support consistent interpretation of project data and transparent compliance checking workflows in structural engineering practice.
