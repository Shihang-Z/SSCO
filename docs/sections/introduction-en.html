Introduction back to ToC

The Sustainable Structural Compliance Ontology (SSCO) has been developed to support sustainability compliance checking in structural engineering within the Architecture, Engineering, Construction, and Operation (AECO) domain. In current practice, the information required for this task is usually distributed across Building Information Modeling (BIM) models, Finite Element Method (FEM)-based structural analysis models, assessment outputs, and requirement documents. This creates a need for a formal semantic model that can connect these information sources and support a consistent interpretation of engineering evidence during compliance assessment. SSCO focuses on the representation of structural entities, analysis-related information, sustainability requirements, and compliance results. The ontology is intended to support knowledge integration and graph-based data representation for compliance checking. It also supports traceable links between project information, assessment evidence, and requirement-oriented evaluation results. In this way, SSCO provides a semantic basis for data integration, knowledge querying, and compliance-oriented reasoning. The conceptualization of SSCO is informed by established international standards. Its building information perspective is aligned with concepts reflected in ISO 16739. Its analysis-related perspective is informed by ISO 10303 resources for finite element analysis and related analysis definitions. Its sustainability and compliance perspective is guided by ISO 21929, ISO 21931, and ISO 37301. These standards provide a structured basis for representing engineering evidence, sustainability criteria, requirement statements, and compliance determination within a coherent ontology framework. The goal of SSCO is to provide a semantic foundation for practical compliance checking in structural engineering. It supports the representation of project data as graph-based knowledge, the linkage between assessment evidence and sustainability requirements, and the generation of explicit compliance results that can be queried and interpreted in a transparent and auditable manner.
